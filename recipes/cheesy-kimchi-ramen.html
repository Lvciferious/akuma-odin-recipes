Cheesy Kimchi Ramen

One of my favourite quick meal/snack habits I've picked up since moving to South Korea is cheesy kimchi ramen. I love ramen in every form, ever since I was a wee lad. But putting cheese on top was something I never did until recently. It's so easy to make that you can go to any GS-25, 7-11, CU-2, etc., pick up a packet of ramen, a single serving of kimchi, fake cheese slices, and make it right there with the in-store microwave. 5 minutes of prep and "cooking", tops. If you're feeling fancy, you can throw in a sausage or a pre-boiled egged, also conveniently bought in the same location. Instant comfort food!

I have the same method every time to make it, because it's so damn easy! The only things I change up on occasion are when I add Furikake rice seasoning or chopped green onions on top. I hope you enjoy this super simple recipe! (Yes, I use fake cheese.)

----------

Ingredients:

  • Any kind of instant ramen packet (x1)
  • 2 slices of fake cheese (think Kraft slices)
  • 1 boiled egg
  • 1 green onion
  • Furikake rice seasoning
  • 2-4 tbsp of kimchi

----------

Steps:

      1.Put packet of ramen and egg a small or medium pot, and fill pot until ramen is submerged.

      2. While water is boiling, wash and chop green onions.

      3. When the water reaches a boil, continue cooking on medium-high heat until noodles have reached desired softness. Remove noodles and let egg boil to desired hardness (more time for hard-boiled, less time for soft boiled.)

      4. Wash noodles with cold water for 30 seconds to 1 minute. This is to prevent the noodles from continuing to cook and going soggy.

      5. Remove egg and run under cold water until it is cool enough to peel. To cool it faster, set it aside in ice water to continue cooling.

      6. Place noodles into a bowl and mix in ramen seasoning with kimchi half of the chopped green onion. Place two slices of fake cheese on top and heat in the microwave.

      7. Peel egg and slice in half. Arrange with ramen. Sprinkle remaining chopped green onions on top. Add Furikake seasoning, if desired.
